Title: Deep learning for the generation of controllable and diverse stories with a latent plan < /i>
Date: June 2023 - August 2023
About

This paper presents a new model for story generation in natural language processing. This model incorporates a deep latent variable approach and uses a high-level model in the form of anchor words to guide the generation process. It combines the advantages of recurrent neural networks (RNN) and transformers to capture both local relationships and global coherence of the generated text. The model includes a linear decoder that follows a sequential order and a non-monotonic decoder for more flexible and creative storytelling. An inference network is also used to control generation according to plan, improving consistency and control of output. Experimental results on a large dataset show that the approach can generate various coherent stories respecting the initial plan. This model opens new perspectives for precise control and creative generation in the field of narrative text generation.

Keywords: Story generation model · Natural language processing · Deep latent variables · Anchor words · Deep learning · Reinforcement learning · Transformers
